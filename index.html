The beginner's guide to watches
01 — Movement
The movement is the engine of the watch. Quartz movements run on a battery and are accurate and affordable. Mechanical movements are powered by a wound spring — either manually or automatically by your wrist's motion. Mechanical is the craft.
02 — Where to Start
Casio F-91W ($15), Seiko 5 SNK809 ($60–80), or Citizen Eco-Drive ($100–150). These are honest watches with real specs. Start here before you spend more.
03 — What to Look For
Dial design, case size (36–40mm is classic), water resistance, and crystal type (sapphire is scratch-resistant). Don't chase brand names. Chase craft.
04 — Go Deeper
Follow Nico Leonard on YouTube. Read Hodinkee. Then come to a UWC meeting and talk to people who've done the research.
